About Andhra Pradesh Politics

Andhra Pradesh, located in the southeastern part of India, has a rich and dynamic political landscape. The state plays a crucial role in India's federal structure, contributing significantly to the Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha, as well as to regional politics.

Key Features of Andhra Pradesh Politics

  • Legislative Assembly (MLAs): Andhra Pradesh has a total of 175 Assembly constituencies, and hence 175 MLAs.
  • Parliamentary Constituencies (MPs): The state has 25 Lok Sabha constituencies and contributes members to the Rajya Sabha.

Recent Elections

  • 2019 General and Assembly Elections: The YSR Congress Party, led by Y. S. Jagan Mohan Reddy, achieved a landslide victory, winning 151 out of 175 Assembly seats and 22 out of 25 Lok Sabha seats.
  • 2024 Elections: The elections are highly anticipated, with significant political competition expected between the YSR Congress Party, Telugu Desam Party (TDP), Jana Sena Party, and other regional and national parties. The Elections held between "YSRCP" & "KUTAMI"(TDP+BJP+JSP) achieved a landslide victory, winning 164 out of 175 Assembly seats and 22 out of 25 Lok Sabha seats.

Major Political Issues

Andhra Pradesh politics revolve around issues such as:

  • Regional development and equitable distribution of resources.
  • The ongoing demand for special category status for the state.
  • Infrastructure development, particularly in the new capital region of Amaravati.
  • Social welfare schemes and rural development.

Role in National Politics

Andhra Pradesh has been a stronghold of regional parties like the YSR Congress Party and the Telugu Desam Party, which have played pivotal roles in coalition politics at the national level.
